Mining New Caledonia

In the late 1800s New Caledonia was discovered to have nickel deposits in its mountains. Between 1890 and 1990 miners moved a half billion tons of rock through opencast mining, involving decapitating the mountains. This caused streams to fill with debris, preventing fishing and navigation. Gravel was dumped on arable land and silt smothered nearby coral reefs. Residents lost their jobs, homes, and land as underpaid immigrants came in to work at the mines. Nearby, smelters caused air pollution, filling the sky with sulfuric fumes. Production only increased with the use of heavy machinery after 1950.
